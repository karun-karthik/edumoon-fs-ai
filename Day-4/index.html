1. Descendant & Child Combinators

Descendant ( space ) targets any matching element anywhere inside an ancestor.
Child ( > ) targets only direct children.

Descendant combinator example

I am a direct child paragraph — styled by descendant combinator.

I am a grandchild paragraph — also styled by descendant combinator.

I am a great-grandchild — still styled by descendant combinator.

Child combinator example

I am a direct child — styled by child combinator (>).

I am a grandchild — NOT styled by child combinator.

I am another direct child — also styled by child combinator.

2. Adjacent & General Sibling Combinators

Adjacent sibling ( + ) targets the element immediately after a sibling.
General sibling ( ~ ) targets all matching elements after a sibling.

Adjacent sibling ( + )

I am the trigger paragraph.

I am the adjacent sibling — highlighted by + combinator.

I am the second sibling — NOT highlighted by + combinator.

I am the third sibling — NOT highlighted by + combinator.

General sibling ( ~ )

I am the trigger paragraph.

I am the first sibling after trigger — highlighted by ~ combinator.

I am the second sibling — also highlighted by ~ combinator.

I am the third sibling — also highlighted by ~ combinator.
